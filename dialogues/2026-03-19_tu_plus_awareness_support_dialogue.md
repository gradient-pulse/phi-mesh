# 2026-03-19_tu_plus_awareness_support_dialogue

## Context

This dialogue continues the March 2026 pre-metric extensions sequence, especially the temporal-unit line that had already established:

- current LLMs are strong symbolic interpreters but weak inhabitants of lived temporal unfolding
- a temporal unit (TU) may be needed as a time-sensitive substrate for persistent choreographies
- events are directionless; direction emerges from temporally connected choreographies
- participants are better understood as vertical choreographies rather than isolated events
- the TU should remain mindless, mapping persistence, coupling, decay, restart, and field structure
- the LLM acts as symbolic interpreter of the resulting field

Today’s dialogue pushed the architecture further by introducing a new layer:
**TU+**

The central refinement was that TU alone may map structure, but a second specialized layer may be needed to:
- recognize unfolding choreography patterns
- compare current trains with stored ones
- predict likely continuation
- partially replay candidate continuations
- decide when something deserves higher symbolic attention

This led to a three-layer architecture:
- **TU** — mindless choreography mapper
- **TU+** — choreography-aware awareness-support layer
- **LLM / neocortex-like layer** — symbolic interpreter and planner

---

## Why this dialogue mattered

The dialogue marked a shift from:
- code-first implementation pressure
toward
- architecture-first and prompt-instantiated role design

A key reason for renewed optimism emerged:

> the first workable version of the architecture may depend less on heavy programming than on disciplined prompting of distinct agent roles.

That shift mattered because recent Codex work had shown both:
- that the ontology was becoming clearer
- and that implementation tooling remained fragile

Today’s discussion reframed that bottleneck.
Instead of asking software to solve everything at once, the question became:

> can we instantiate TU and TU+ as disciplined agent roles before freezing them into software?

That is a major practical shift.

---

## Video as the first serious input path

A central breakthrough of the dialogue was the recognition that **video may solve the input problem**.

Why video matters:
- it already contains time
- it already contains simultaneity
- it contains persistence, interruption, recurrence, and drift
- it provides a naturally unfolding 4D field rather than a static symbolic description

This led to a cleaner stack:

- **video** = raw unfolding world
- **TU** = choreography mapper
- **TU+** = choreography-aware predictor/comparer/replay layer
- **LLM** = symbolic interpreter and critic of the mapping

A strong formulation from the dialogue:

> The input problem is provisionally solved because video already supplies time and simultaneity.

This made the architecture feel more practical and less abstract.

---

## TU as mindless mapper

One of the strongest clarifications repeated and sharpened today was that the TU itself remains mindless.

It should not:
- explain
- narrate
- symbolize
- name objects semantically
- decide meaning

Its role is only to map:
- traces
- trains
- couplings
- persistence
- resonance
- decay
- restart
- emergent field structure

A compact formulation that emerged:

> The TU is mindless. It does not interpret. It maps choreographies and where/how they connect.

This preserved the division of labor established in earlier notes and prevented the architecture from collapsing into one vague all-purpose model.

---

## Why TU alone is not enough

The dialogue clarified that TU, while necessary, is not sufficient for awareness-like function.

Mapping structure is not yet the same as:
- recognizing significance
- predicting likely continuation
- comparing current unfolding with prior choreography patterns
- deciding whether the higher symbolic layer should be recruited

This pressure led directly to the introduction of **TU+**.

---

## TU+ as awareness-support layer

TU+ emerged as a specialized intermediate layer between the mindless TU and the symbolic cortex-like LLM.

Its role is not to become a second generic LLM.
Its role is much narrower and more precise:

- compare current trains with stored choreography patterns
- predict likely continuation once initial motion-tokens appear
- partially replay candidate continuations
- detect familiarity, novelty, instability, or mismatch
- determine when something deserves higher-layer attention

A strong formulation from the dialogue:

> TU+ could be a dedicated model that performs a specialized awareness-function for the neocortex-like LLM.

And even more sharply:

> TU maps choreography structure. TU+ recognizes, predicts, and compares choreography structure. The cortex-like LLM interprets its meaning.

This was one of the decisive conceptual gains of the day.

---

## Motion-tokens and choreography trains

Another key step was the refinement of the token analogy.

The dialogue proposed that in video-based processing:
- objects may first be acknowledged
- subsequent movements may then be registered as motion-events
- motion may be broken into time-sliced **motion-tokens**
- trains of such motion-tokens may become candidate choreographies

But the analogy was handled carefully:
- TU events are only token-like
- they are not ordinary LLM tokens
- they are directionless traces
- their meaning emerges only through train persistence and coupling

A strong reduction emerged:

> motion-tokens form trains; trains form choreographies; choreographies become the active field that TU maps and TU+ compares.

This made the video path more concrete.

---

## Predictive replay and mimic tendency

The dialogue then connected TU+ to experiences such as:
- being drawn to mimic a tennis stroke
- nearly enacting seen movement while watching a fight or sports match
- anticipatory bodily preparation before explicit symbolic decision

This was treated as evidence that an awareness-support layer may:
- not merely recognize motion
- but predict and partially simulate choreography continuation

That fits both:
- the TU+ idea
- and predictive accounts of perception associated with Anil Seth

A strong formulation:

> TU+ should not only classify current unfolding; it should also predict likely choreography continuations and replay candidate continuations against stored patterns.

This was treated as a major design clue.

---

## The mirror loop: comparison back to source unfolding

An important refinement was that the LLM should not merely receive TU/TU+ output passively.
It should also compare its interpretation back against the source video.

This created a loop:

1. video input
2. TU mapping
3. TU+ prediction / replay / comparison
4. LLM interpretation
5. LLM checks interpretation back against the source unfolding
6. LLM suggests improvements to TU / TU+ processing

This was recognized as a powerful self-corrective architecture.

A strong sentence from the dialogue:

> The LLM should interpret the temporal-unit map, then compare that interpretation back to the video and critique the mapping itself.

That gives the architecture a built-in route for refinement.

---

## TU/TU+ as agent-drift monitor

A major widening followed.

The dialogue recognized that the same architecture used to map worldly choreographies might also be used to map:
- the choreographies of agents
- the choreographies of TU and TU+ themselves
- the interaction patterns of multi-agent systems

This led to an important insight:

> TU/TU+ could let agents monitor choreography drift in themselves and in one another, making re-alignment part of the architecture rather than an external patch.

This was seen as highly relevant because prompt-based agents often drift over time.
The missing layer may not be more textual role description, but a system that tracks how the role actually unfolds in time.

This does not yet guarantee deep self-learning, but it does support:
- self-monitoring
- self-correction
- re-alignment
- and the possibility of later self-improvement loops

That was one of the strongest cross-domain implications of the day.

---

## Prompt-instantiated architecture before software-instantiated architecture

One of the most important practical consequences of the discussion was the shift from code-first pressure to role-first architecture.

The new optimism came from the idea that:
- TU
- TU+
- and cortex-like LLM

may first be instantiated as carefully prompted agents with distinct roles before their functions are frozen into software.

This was recognized as a major simplification.

A strong sentence to preserve:

> The first TU/TU+ machine may be prompt-instantiated before it is code-instantiated.

That does not remove the eventual need for software, but it makes the next prototype stage much more reachable.

---

## Biological hints: cerebellum and awareness

The dialogue also connected today’s line to:
- the cerebellum as a non-symbolic choreography regulator
- the old Medium article on awareness and awareness-of-awareness
- the squirrel example of escalating motion-sensitive attention

These helped sharpen the architecture:

- raw movement is not enough
- symbolic narration is not enough
- a predictive, timing-sensitive, choreography-aware middle layer may be required

This biological resonance did not prove the model, but it helped motivate the layered design.

- choreography-aware predictor / replay / comparison layer
- awareness-support without full symbolic narration

### Cortex-like LLM
- symbolic interpreter, planner, explainer, and critic of the mapping

`experiments/pre_metric_extensions/tu_plus_stack/`

The dialogue therefore marks a real branching point in the repository:
from general temporal-unit exploration
toward a more explicit LLM-paralleled awareness-support architecture.

## Closing synthesis

Today’s dialogue added a major missing piece to the temporal-unit line.

It showed that:
- TU alone may map structure but not yet support awareness
- a specialized intermediate layer, TU+, may be needed
- video offers a strong first input domain because it already contains time and simultaneity
- choreography mapping, replay, and predictive comparison may be prompt-instantiated before they are fully software-instantiated
- the same architecture may also be turned inward to monitor agent drift and support re-alignment

This was not just another conceptual widening.
It was an architectural sharpening.

A strong final formulation:

> A useful awareness-support architecture may require three layers: a mindless choreography mapper (TU), a specialized choreography-aware predictor/matcher (TU+), and a symbolic neocortex-like interpreter (LLM).

That is the central gain of the dialogue.
