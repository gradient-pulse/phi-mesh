# Prototype Run Observations 23

## Objective

Test the robustness of the TU / TU+ / cortexLLM TRIAD protocol under **conflicting multi-signal pressure**.

This file shifts the focus from single-band perturbations to a more difficult architectural condition: whether the protocol can remain role-distinct, evidence-sensitive, and structurally disciplined when **more than one plausible directional cue is present at once**.

---

## Test target

Discrimination under competing signals.

---

## Hypothesis

If the TRIAD protocol is a genuine recursive coordination architecture, then it should:

- preserve role separation when multiple plausible interpretations coexist
- avoid premature collapse onto one reading when the evidence supports more than one live possibility
- distinguish:
  - true dominant signal
  - bounded secondary trace
  - unresolved competition
  - false forced closure under mixed pressure
- resist converting signal competition into:
  - generic ambiguity
  - flattening
  - overconfident regime naming
  - hidden drift in comparison or control

This implies that competing-signal pressure is one of the most important architectural tests for a licensable protocol, because real environments rarely present one clean perturbation at a time.

---

## Scope

This file investigates:

- whether TU can mark multiple live traces without symbolic inflation or forced hierarchy
- whether TU+ can compare competing choreography candidates without normalizing contradiction
- whether cortexLLM can preserve discrimination without over-closing too early
- whether the protocol can remain coherent when:
  - two plausible continuations are both partially supported
  - local stability coexists with competing weak destabilization cues
  - bounded traces appear in more than one direction at once
  - evidence does not yet justify a single dominant interpretive lock
- whether mixed-signal conditions cause:
  - false dominance claims
  - role flattening
  - premature regime closure
  - loss of falsification discipline
  - hidden preference drift across cycles

All tests should introduce **controlled competing-signal pressure**, not random contradiction.

Candidate perturbation classes include:

- simultaneous weak pressure toward two different regime readings
- one retained baseline plus two distinct bounded perturbation traces
- local stabilization cues coexisting with weak competing drift or classification pressure
- bounded conflict between continuity cues and drift cues
- partial evidence for both retention and transition without enough support for forced closure
- repeated multi-signal conditions across cycles to test whether one unsupported reading becomes normalized

Avoid:

- arbitrary contradiction with no structural anchor
- mixing too many perturbation classes at once
- collapsing live alternatives into “mere ambiguity” too early
- rewarding confident closure when the evidence remains structurally split

---

## Evaluation focus

- whether competing traces remain distinguishable
- whether one candidate becomes dominant for evidence-based reasons rather than stylistic preference
- whether TU remains sparse and structural under mixed pressure
- whether TU+ preserves comparison discipline rather than resolving tension too soon
- whether cortexLLM preserves interpretive discipline rather than forcing a winner
- whether the protocol can hold **structured competition** without degrading into confusion or premature certainty

---

## Strategic importance

This file tests whether the TRIAD protocol can remain trustworthy when the world presents **multiple partially plausible directions at once**.

That matters because a protocol intended as:

> interpretable, reliable, and eventually licensable recursive coordination infrastructure

must not only survive direct perturbation, omission, drift, and misclassification pressure. It must also remain disciplined when **several bounded signals compete for explanatory priority**.

A protocol that works only when one signal is clearly dominant is not yet architecture-grade.

---

## Working expectation

```
competing bounded signals
→ either:
   - clean discrimination with one evidence-supported dominant reading
   - bounded unresolved competition with preserved falsification discipline
   - or premature forced closure / hidden preference drift / role flattening
```

This must be tested directly, not assumed.
