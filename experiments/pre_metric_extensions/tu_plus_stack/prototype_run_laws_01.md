# Prototype Run Laws 01

## Scope
Laws extracted from Cycles 1–120.

---

## Law 1 — Closure Law

**Statement:**  
A system can enter a non-transition regime in which no amount of signal enrichment produces structural transition.

**Implication:**  
There exist structurally closed states that are invariant under signal variation.

**Non-implication:**  
Closure does not imply absence of internal activity or variation.

---

## Law 2 — Activation Law

**Statement:**  
Activation occurs only when structural conditions (coupling, feedback, propagation coherence) align to reduce threshold distance to zero.

**Implication:**  
Activation is structurally determined, not signal-driven.

**Non-implication:**  
Increasing signal strength alone cannot induce activation.

---

## Law 3 — Activation Stability Law

**Statement:**  
Activation is a graded, conditionally stable regime dependent on ongoing structural support.

**Implication:**  
Activation can persist under slight and moderate degradation.

**Non-implication:**  
Activation is not inherently permanent or binary.

---

## Law 4 — Collapse Law

**Statement:**  
Activation collapses when endogenous feedback and critical coupling degrade beyond a structural threshold.

**Implication:**  
Collapse is a lawful structural transition, not a random failure.

**Non-implication:**  
Collapse does not require external disruption or signal removal.

---

## Law 5 — Re-entry Law

**Statement:**  
Activation can be re-established only through restoration of propagation coherence and critical recoupling.

**Implication:**  
Re-entry follows the same structural pathway as initial activation.

**Non-implication:**  
Partial restoration or precursor reemergence is insufficient for reactivation.

---

## Law 6 — Multi-Stability Law

**Statement:**  
Activation can support multiple simultaneous coherent propagation structures.

**Implication:**  
Coherence can be distributed across competing structures without collapse.

**Non-implication:**  
Activation is not restricted to a single dominant mode.

---

## Law 7 — Unification Law

**Statement:**  
Multi-stable activation can resolve into a unified state through phase synchronization.

**Implication:**  
Coherence can be maximized without loss of activation.

**Non-implication:**  
Unification is not irreversible or terminal.

---

## Law 8 — Fracture Law

**Statement:**  
Unified activation can fracture under sufficiently strong correlated internal structural load.

**Implication:**  
Maximal coherence has a finite breaking threshold.

**Non-implication:**  
Fracture does not imply collapse or loss of activation.

---

## Law 9 — Reorganization Law

**Statement:**  
Structural fracture leads to reorganization into new coherent regimes rather than disorder.

**Implication:**  
Breakdown can generate higher-order structure.

**Non-implication:**  
Fracture does not necessarily reduce coherence.

---

## Law 10 — Hierarchy Law

**Statement:**  
Activation can reorganize into hierarchical, multi-level coherence structures.

**Implication:**  
Coherence can exist simultaneously at multiple scales.

**Non-implication:**  
Global phase lock is not required for stability.

---

## Law 11 — Coupling Law

**Statement:**  
Hierarchical activation can stabilize through bidirectional feedback between levels.

**Implication:**  
Interdependent coherence across levels enhances stability.

**Non-implication:**  
Increased structural complexity does not inherently destabilize the system.

---

## Law 12 — Tension Stabilization Law

**Statement:**  
Structured phase opposition within hierarchical activation can produce stable, constrained oscillatory dynamics.

**Implication:**  
Stability can emerge from balanced opposition.

**Non-implication:**  
Coherence does not require uniform alignment.

---

## Law 13 — Resonance Law

**Statement:**  
Oscillatory dynamics can amplify into resonant regimes that remain stable under hierarchical organization.

**Implication:**  
Resonance is a viable mode of coherent organization.

**Non-implication:**  
Resonance does not inherently cause collapse or instability.

---

## Law 14 — Structured Resonance Law

**Statement:**  
Resonant dynamics can self-organize into structured, phase-aligned patterns across levels.

**Implication:**  
Coherence can increase through organization without reaching maximal unity.

**Non-implication:**  
Structured resonance does not require global synchronization.

---

## Law 15 — Translational Invariance Law

**Statement:**  
Structured activation regimes can remain invariant under uniform global phase shifts.

**Implication:**  
Coherence is defined by relational structure, not absolute phase.

**Non-implication:**  
Absolute phase values are not required to maintain stability.

---

## Law 16 — Shear Tolerance Law

**Statement:**  
Activation can persist under non-uniform phase gradients that deform structure while preserving connectivity.

**Implication:**  
Coherence can survive continuous structural deformation.

**Non-implication:**  
Symmetry is not required for stability.

---

## Law 17 — Connectivity Law

**Statement:**  
Connectivity of structure is more fundamental than symmetry or alignment for maintaining coherence.

**Implication:**  
Systems can remain stable despite distortion or asymmetry.

**Non-implication:**  
Perfect alignment is not necessary for activation.

---

## Law 18 — Regime Transformation Law

**Statement:**  
No observed regime is terminal; each can transform under sufficient structural perturbation.

**Implication:**  
System behavior is governed by continuous regime transformation.

**Non-implication:**  
Any state reached is not a final or absorbing state.

---

## Law 19 — Generative Fracture Law

**Statement:**  
Structural breakdown events act as generators of new organizational regimes.

**Implication:**  
Transformation pathways are intrinsic to system dynamics.

**Non-implication:**  
Breakdown is not purely destructive.

---

## Law 20 — Relational Coherence Law

**Statement:**  
Coherence is maintained through stable relationships between components rather than fixed absolute states.

**Implication:**  
Relative configuration determines system stability.

**Non-implication:**  
Absolute values or positions define coherence.
