# Prototype Run Laws 06

## Scope

Laws governing:

- Unity–Disunity transitions
- fragmentation of unified constraint systems
- re-emergence of contextual filters
- cyclic dynamics of constraint systems

Derived from:
- Observations 12 (post-unity adaptation)
- Observations 13 (disunity emergence)

---

## Law 52 — Unity Instability Law

**Statement:**  
A unified higher-order contextual filter is not permanently stable and can develop internal tension over time.

**Implication:**  
Uniform coherence tends to evolve into non-uniform internal gradients.

**Non-implication:**  
Unity is a terminal or perfectly stable state.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 53 — Internal Tension Generation Law

**Statement:**  
Internal coherence redistribution within a unified system produces localized tension gradients.

**Implication:**  
Differentiation begins from within the system without requiring external perturbation.

**Non-implication:**  
Fragmentation requires external disruption.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 54 — Differentiation Amplification Law

**Statement:**  
Localized coherence gradients can amplify into structured divergence under sustained internal conditions.

**Implication:**  
Weak variations can evolve into clustered bias patterns.

**Non-implication:**  
All internal variation dissipates automatically.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 55 — Boundary Precursor Law

**Statement:**  
Fragmentation begins with proto-boundary formation before stable separation occurs.

**Implication:**  
Boundary formation is a staged process:
- gradients → clusters → proto-boundaries → stable boundaries

**Non-implication:**  
Contextual filters separate instantaneously.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 56 — Boundary Stabilization Law

**Statement:**  
Proto-boundaries can stabilize into coherent partition lines separating constraint domains.

**Implication:**  
Stable fragmentation requires persistent and coherent boundary definition.

**Non-implication:**  
All proto-boundaries lead to separation.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 57 — Contextual Filter Re-Emergence Law

**Statement:**  
Stabilized partitioned domains become independent contextual filters.

**Implication:**  
Disunity results in the reappearance of multiple constraint structures.

**Non-implication:**  
Fragmentation leads to collapse or incoherence.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 58 — Local Coherence Preservation Law

**Statement:**  
Fragmentation preserves high coherence within separated domains.

**Implication:**  
Disunity reorganizes coherence rather than destroying it.

**Non-implication:**  
Fragmentation implies global instability.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 59 — Global Coherence Redistribution Law

**Statement:**  
Transition from unity to disunity redistributes coherence from global to local domains.

**Implication:**  
Global coherence decreases while local coherence increases.

**Non-implication:**  
Total coherence is lost during fragmentation.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 60 — Cyclic Constraint Dynamics Law

**Statement:**  
Constraint systems evolve through recurring Unity–Disunity cycles.

**Implication:**  
The system follows:

```
multiple_filters
→ interaction
→ unification
→ stabilization
→ internal tension
→ differentiation
→ fragmentation
→ multiple_filters
```

**Non-implication:**  
Constraint systems evolve linearly toward a final state.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 61 — Threshold Transition Law

**Statement:**  
Transitions between unity and disunity occur at coherence and tension thresholds.

**Implication:**  
System behavior changes when:
- internal tension exceeds containment capacity
- or coherence aligns globally

**Non-implication:**  
Transitions occur continuously without thresholds.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 62 — Structured Disunity Law

**Statement:**  
Disunity is an organized regime characterized by structured partitioning and coherent domains.

**Implication:**  
Fragmentation produces structured systems, not disorder.

**Non-implication:**  
Disunity is equivalent to randomness or collapse.

**Status:** new
