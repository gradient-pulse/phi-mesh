# Prototype Run Observations 16

## Objective

Test the robustness, perturbation response, and boundary conditions of the TU / TU+ / cortexLLM triadic protocol now that cyclic regime structure, re-unification, staged restart, and local-domain closure have been established.

This file shifts the emphasis from discovery of core regime structure to evaluation of protocol reliability under stress, ambiguity, and controlled disruption.

---

## Test target

Robustness and failure boundaries of staged triadic coordination.

---

## Hypothesis

If the TRIAD protocol is functioning as a genuine role-separated coordination architecture rather than a loose prompt artifact, then it should:

- preserve role distinction under moderate perturbation
- maintain interpretable regime structure under stress
- show identifiable failure signatures rather than arbitrary collapse
- recover from disruption through lawful pathways rather than generic drift
- reveal boundary conditions where the protocol degrades, fuses, fragments, or misclassifies state

This implies that robustness testing must now become primary.

---

## Scope

This file investigates:

- persistence of role separation under perturbation
- sensitivity of TU, TU+, and cortexLLM to overlap, corruption, or pressure
- degradation modes of staged coordination
- false unification, false fragmentation, and false restart conditions
- recovery behavior after controlled disturbance
- whether previously established cyclic laws remain stable under altered input conditions

All tests should begin from already validated protocol structure and use targeted perturbations rather than exploratory free variation.

Potential perturbation classes include:

- incompatible overlap
- noisy returned traces
- role-boundary pressure
- downward-bias conflict
- memory corruption or partial omission
- restart pressure under ambiguous state
- local-domain interaction under stress

Avoid:

- re-running already validated baseline cycles without perturbation value
- treating ordinary variation as robustness evidence
- introducing multiple uncontrolled perturbations in one cycle
- confusing protocol breakdown with meaningful new law formation

---

## Evaluation focus

- role-boundary persistence
- regime-classification stability
- recovery vs collapse
- path dependence under perturbation
- detectability of failure onset
- whether perturbation reveals new lawful failure classes

---

## Strategic importance

This file determines whether the TRIAD protocol is:

- merely descriptive under curated conditions
- or
- robust enough to be treated as an engineering protocol for recursive coordination

This is critical for moving from:

> observed structured behavior

to

> interpretable, testable protocol behavior under stress
