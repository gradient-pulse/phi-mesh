# T-Protocol — Glossary v1

## Purpose

This glossary provides compact working definitions for the key terms used in the T-Protocol package.

Its purpose is to reduce misunderstanding and keep the package readable by:

- licensees
- reviewers
- implementers
- technical partners
- strategic evaluators

Definitions here are protocol-facing rather than purely philosophical.

---

## Source / object hypothesis

A provisional identity hypothesis for a persistent emitter or carrier of observable change.

It is treated as:
- provisional
- revisable
- stabilizable
- splittable
- dissolvable

Compactly:

> A source/object is first a hypothesis, then motion and persistence either stabilize or revise it.

---

## Motion-token

A primitive spatiotemporal or process trace bundle.

It is not a narrative unit and not yet a high-level interpretation.

Typical ingredients include:
- source reference
- time slice
- displacement or local change
- direction or angular change
- relation/contact change
- confidence

Compactly:

> A motion-token is a primitive processable trace bundle.

---

## Train

A temporally persistent sequence of motion-tokens associated with one continuing source/object hypothesis.

A train may:
- persist
- strengthen
- weaken
- branch
- decay
- restart

Compactly:

> A train is the persistent local sequence through which motion-tokens begin to become choreography.

---

## Coupling

Structured simultaneous or near-simultaneous unfolding across trains, within a coherence window, sufficient to support a relational reading.

Coupling is not:
- mere proximity
- mere resemblance
- mere symbolic association

A coupling may:
- emerge
- strengthen
- stabilize
- weaken
- fragment
- dissolve

Compactly:

> Coupling is structured simultaneous or near-simultaneous unfolding across trains, within a coherence window, sufficient to support a relational reading.

---

## Choreography

A coupled or otherwise persistently organized cluster of one or more trains with viable unfolding structure.

Choreography is the pattern-level unit over which TU+ compares, replays, and senses likely continuation.

Compactly:

> Choreography begins when trains persist and organize strongly enough to form a coherent unfolding structure.

---

## Stored choreography memory

Previously stabilized choreography forms available for replay and comparison.

This is pattern memory retained near the action- and comparison-facing side of the protocol.

Compactly:

> Stored choreography memory is replay-available pattern memory.

---

## Dynamic choreography memory

The memory already carried by sustained live choreography.

Persistent trains and couplings do not merely record what has happened. They also constrain what can likely happen next.

Compactly:

> Sustained choreography is not only present structure; it is dynamic memory shaping likely continuation.

---

## Coherence

The degree to which trains and choreographies remain viably coupled as a whole across unfolding time.

Coherence is not mere smoothness or familiarity. It concerns the viability of organized relation under change.

Compactly:

> Coherence is viable held-togetherness across unfolding time.

---

## Mismatch

A structured delta between predicted and returned unfolding.

Mismatch is not merely “error.” It is a coherence-relevant difference between:
- expected continuation
and
- observed continuation

Mismatch may trigger:
- mild revision
- ambiguity preservation
- de-confirmation
- restart

Compactly:

> Mismatch is the structured delta between predicted and returned unfolding.

---

## Returned trace

Observed return evidence after continuation or enactment.

Returned traces keep the protocol answerable to unfolding reality rather than allowing it to remain self-sealed.

Compactly:

> Returned traces are the protocol’s correction channel back from unfolding consequence.

---

## Replay

The structured reactivation of relevant choreography memory for comparison against the current field.

Replay is not theatrical reenactment.  
It is pattern reactivation in support of comparison and continuation sensing.

Compactly:

> Replay is memory reactivation for continuation judgment.

---

## Continuation sensing

The TU+ function of sensing likely next train structure by balancing:

- stored choreography memory
- dynamic choreography memory
- current field state
- mismatch history
- bounded contextual bias

This is broader and more continuity-sensitive than ordinary prediction.

Compactly:

> Continuation sensing is replay-grounded, mismatch-accountable sensing of likely next unfolding.

---

## TU

The structural mapping role.

TU:
- maps hypotheses
- emits motion-tokens
- extends trains
- registers coupling candidates
- updates low-level field structure

TU does not:
- narrate meaning
- interpret significance
- plan symbolically

Compactly:

> TU maps structure only.

---

## TU+

The choreography-aware comparison, replay, and continuation-sensing role.

TU+:
- compares current field against choreography memory
- detects novelty, instability, and mismatch
- projects candidate continuations
- raises attention when needed

Compactly:

> TU+ compares, replays, senses likely continuation, and flags.

---

## cortexLLM

The symbolic interpretation and bounded downward-bias role.

cortexLLM:
- frames context
- preserves ambiguity where warranted
- sets hold/act/reorient stance
- sends bounded contextual pressure downward

It does not remap low-level structure directly.

Compactly:

> cortexLLM interprets and biases without low-level overwrite.

---

## Downward bias

Compact contextual pressure sent from cortexLLM downward into the field.

Examples may include:
- attend
- compare
- hold
- reorient
- sustain
- suppress
- act

Compactly:

> Downward bias is bounded contextual pressure, not structural overwrite.

---

## Attention state

The current salience and recruitment condition of the field.

It tracks whether the field should:
- remain background
- be monitored
- recruit higher symbolic attention

Compactly:

> Attention state marks when the field deserves stronger scrutiny.

---

## Shared state

The live structured memory of the choreography field, preserved across cycles and read differently by TU, TU+, and cortexLLM.

It is:
- persistent
- role-bounded
- partially updated
- recursion-friendly

Compactly:

> The shared state is the live structured memory of the field across cycles.

---

## Partial update

The rule that shared state evolves by structured reweighting and selective field revision, not by total rewrite.

Compactly:

> The field evolves by partial update, not total replacement.

---

## Restart

Re-initiation of a mainline after contradiction, collapse, or de-confirmation.

Restart is not forgetting.
It preserves enough history to remain intelligible.

Compactly:

> Restart means re-initiation with preserved history, not amnesia.

---

## Weak recoupling

Fresh weak relational emergence after restart.

Weak recoupling must be distinguished from automatic revival of a previously collapsed joint field.

Compactly:

> Weak recoupling is fresh renewed relation, not memory-driven false reopening.

---

## Non-forced promotion

The rule that a dominant reading may not be promoted merely because it is narratively attractive or symbolically convenient.

Promotion must follow structural support.

Compactly:

> Promotion must follow the field, not rhetorical convenience.

---

## Contradiction preservation

The rule that mutually exclusive continuations remain separable when evidence does not justify reconciliation.

Compactly:

> Contradiction should remain explicit where closure is not yet warranted.

---

## Faithful implementation

An implementation in which the bounded recursive triadic grammar of T-Protocol is materially preserved in:

- runtime
- state
- transitions
- correction
- role discipline
- validation behavior

Compactly:

> Faithful implementation means the protocol is actually present in substance, not only in wording.

---

## Conformance

Behavioral and structural preservation of T-Protocol under named pressure tests.

Conformance is not just having the right files; it is behaving correctly under review.

Compactly:

> Conformance is behavioral proof that the protocol remains itself.

---

## PoHCCP

Principle of Highest Contextual Coherence Path.

This is the current working name, in protocol language, for the deeper coherence-selective principle guiding TU+ continuation sensing.

Compactly:

> PoHCCP is the principle that TU+ follows when sensing what comes next: the path of highest contextual coherence through transformation.

---

## Closing statement

This glossary should be read as a working implementation aid.

A compact final formulation:

> The glossary exists to keep T-Protocol readable, buildable, and reviewable without losing the distinctions that define it.
