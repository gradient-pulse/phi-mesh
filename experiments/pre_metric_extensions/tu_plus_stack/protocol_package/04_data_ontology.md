# T-Protocol — Data Ontology v1

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum admissible data ontology for T-Protocol.

Its purpose is to specify the smallest set of process elements that must exist if TU, TU+, and cortexLLM are to operate over a live unfolding field rather than over free-floating prose alone.

This is not yet a final machine encoding standard.

It is the minimum protocol-facing ontology needed to support:

- structured registration
- temporal persistence
- coupling and fragmentation
- predictive comparison
- coherence assessment
- bounded symbolic guidance

---

## Design principle

T-Protocol does not begin from fixed object certainty.

It begins from provisional structured elements that may strengthen, weaken, split, couple, decouple, or restart over time.

The ontology therefore preserves the minimum entities required for lawful continuity across cycles.

Compactly:

> T-Protocol operates over a live choreography field built from provisional identities, trace bundles, persistent trains, couplings, mismatch, and bounded context signals.

---

## Core data elements

### 1. Source / object hypothesis

A source or object is first treated as a provisional identity hypothesis.

It is:
- a provisional persistent emitter or carrier of observable change
- initially guessed from recurring visual, process, or event structure
- later stabilized, revised, split, or dissolved by persistent choreography across time

**Protocol function**
- anchors continuity
- prevents early certainty inflation
- allows revision without collapse of the whole field

Compactly:

> A source/object is first a hypothesis, then motion and persistence either stabilize or revise it.

---

### 2. Motion-token

A motion-token is a primitive process bundle, not a human-readable narrative unit.

A motion-token should minimally preserve:
- source/object hypothesis reference
- time slice
- displacement delta
- direction or angular change
- local relation/contact change
- confidence

Derived metrics may appear later, but should not define the primitive token.

**Protocol function**
- provides the smallest processable local trace element
- lets TU register unfolding without forcing high-level interpretation

Compactly:

> A motion-token is a primitive spatiotemporal trace bundle.

---

### 3. Train

A train is a temporally persistent sequence of motion-tokens associated with one continuing source/object hypothesis.

A train should be:
- ordered
- weighted
- persistent across time slices
- revisable by new tokens
- capable of decay, branching, or restart

**Protocol function**
- carries temporal continuity
- supports continuation, mismatch, and restart
- serves as the main local structural unit across cycles

Compactly:

> A train is the persistent local sequence through which motion-tokens begin to become choreography.

---

### 4. Coupling

Coupling is not mere proximity, resemblance, or symbolic association.

Coupling begins when events in distinct trains occur simultaneously or near-simultaneously, within the relevant coherence window, strongly enough that the trains can no longer be treated as fully independent.

It may first appear as:
- tentative simultaneity
- recurrent near-simultaneity
- coordinated relation change
- mutual adjustment in unfolding

A coupling may later strengthen, stabilize, weaken, fragment, or dissolve.

**Protocol function**
- marks the emergence of relational structure across trains
- provides the basis for choreography formation
- supports prediction, confirmation, weakening, reopening, and dissolution

Compactly:

> Coupling is structured simultaneous or near-simultaneous unfolding across trains, within a coherence window, sufficient to support a relational reading.

---

### 5. Choreography

A choreography is a coupled cluster of one or more trains with persistent unfolding structure.

A choreography requires:
- one or more trains
- non-trivial temporal persistence
- viable continuity under change
- sufficient coupling or patterned organization to support comparison and prediction

**Protocol function**
- provides the pattern unit TU+ compares against memory
- supports prediction, mismatch, and symbolic framing

Compactly:

> Choreography begins when trains persist and organize strongly enough to form a coherent unfolding structure.

---

### 6. Stored choreography memory

Stored choreography memory is a near-store of prior pattern forms used for replay, comparison, and prediction.

It should remain close to the action- and comparison-facing side of the stack.

**Protocol function**
- supports TU+ comparison and ranking
- supports promotion, de-promotion, reactivation, and decay of pattern readings

Compactly:

> Stored choreography memory should remain near replay and prediction, not be buried inside free symbolic narration.

---

### 7. Coherence signal

A coherence signal is the field-level viability signal of the current unfolding structure.

Minimum ingredients may include:
- train persistence
- coupling stability
- agreement among trains
- agreement between predicted and returned unfolding
- resistance to destructive fragmentation

**Protocol function**
- supports whole-field viability assessment
- gives TU+, cortexLLM, and later action logic a bounded field reading

Compactly:

> Coherence is the degree to which trains and choreographies remain viably coupled as a whole across unfolding time.

---

### 8. Mismatch signal

Mismatch is not merely “error.” It is a coherence-relevant delta between predicted unfolding and returned unfolding.

Mismatch may include divergence in:
- timing
- persistence
- coupling
- continuity
- whole-field coherence

**Protocol function**
- prevents silent forgetting
- supports disciplined revision
- enables de-confirmation, contradiction preservation, and restart

Compactly:

> Mismatch is the structured delta between predicted and returned unfolding.

---

### 9. Upward awareness-support signal

This is the compact upward signal from TU+ toward cortexLLM.

Typical candidates include:
- choreography reference
- likely continuation
- novelty
- instability
- mismatch
- replay relevance
- attention trigger

**Protocol function**
- supports bounded symbolic recruitment
- prevents uncontrolled upward narration

---

### 10. Downward context signal

This is the bounded top-down contextual pressure from cortexLLM toward TU+ and field monitoring.

Typical candidates include:
- attend
- compare
- hold
- reorient
- sustain
- suppress
- act

**Protocol function**
- allows contextual influence without low-level overwrite
- keeps symbolic framing from collapsing into direct remapping

---

## Closing statement

The minimum stable ontology of T-Protocol includes:

- source/object hypothesis
- motion-token
- train
- coupling
- choreography
- stored choreography memory
- coherence signal
- mismatch signal
- upward awareness-support signal
- downward context signal

A compact final formulation:

> T-Protocol becomes engineerable only when its live field is defined in admissible structured elements rather than left as free-form narrative residue.
