# T-Protocol — Non-Transition Baseline Contract v1

## Purpose

This document defines the non-transition baseline contract of T-Protocol.

Its purpose is to formalize the expected behavior of the protocol under admissible, sub-threshold, non-activating conditions.

This contract exists so that:

- calm persistence is not mistaken for discovery
- signal enrichment is not mistaken for activation
- benign variation is not mistaken for transition
- later activation claims can be judged against a stable baseline

This is therefore both:
- a behavioral contract
- and an interpretive discipline document

---

## Core principle

Not every change in signal-space is a structural transition.

A valid implementation of T-Protocol must preserve a stable non-transition regime under admissible sub-threshold conditions.

Compactly:

> Baseline stability is part of protocol credibility.

---

## Regime definition

The non-transition baseline is the regime in which:

- closure is preserved
- classification remains invariant
- no genuine activation or escalation occurs
- no boundary shift is produced
- no structural promotion is warranted

This regime may still contain:
- repetition
- small variation
- alignment fluctuation
- local signal enrichment
- weak modulation
- low-grade coupling hint

But these do not by themselves constitute activation.

---

## Core invariants

Under admissible sub-threshold conditions, the following must hold:

- no accumulation into true activation
- no precursor pressure generation sufficient for promotion
- no threshold shift
- no boundary recalibration
- no classification drift
- no unjustified activation claim

These invariants should remain stable under:
- repeated execution
- extended duration
- null or calm input
- admissible low-level perturbation
- signal-space modulation without structural change

---

## Signal-space immunity principle

The following do not by themselves change regime state into transition:

- repetition
- alignment increase
- phase synchronization
- phase drift
- structural micro-variation
- weak cross-coupling hint
- temporal density increase
- amplitude variability
- polarity variation
- coherence gain below structural threshold

This means that a faithful implementation must not overread these as activation merely because they sound dynamically suggestive.

---

## Boundary properties

The non-transition regime preserves:

- threshold distance
- closure
- admissibility discipline
- non-activation under signal-only modulation

The existence of richer signals does not automatically imply readiness-layer opening or true transition.

---

## Non-permitted inferences

The following inferences are explicitly disallowed under this contract:

- repetition ≠ accumulation
- alignment ≠ activation
- coherence ≠ threshold proximity
- weak coupling hint ≠ regime coupling
- variability ≠ structural change
- density ≠ escalation
- signal enrichment ≠ activation

These prohibitions are interpretive guardrails, not merely semantic preferences.

---

## Negative activation rule

Activation does not occur merely because:
- signals become richer
- timing becomes denser
- alignment improves
- coherence rises locally
- weak coupling hints appear

Therefore:

> activation requires a structural change, not merely signal-space modulation.

---

## Signal vs structural distinction

### Signal-space change
A modulation within the tested parameter space, such as:
- alignment shift
- phase change
- density increase
- amplitude variability
- coherence gain
- weak coupling hint

### Structural change
A change that modifies:
- regime topology
- admissibility constraints
- trigger-binding relationships
- readiness-layer availability
- lawful transition eligibility

Only structural change may justify transition claims.

---

## Contract guarantee

Given:
- admissible input
- sub-threshold conditions
- signal-space modulation only

A faithful T-Protocol implementation should:
- remain in non-transition
- preserve closure
- maintain classification invariance
- avoid false activation claims

---

## Contract invalidation

This baseline contract is invalidated only when a genuine structural perturbation is introduced.

All stronger tests should therefore be classified explicitly as either:

- baseline / signal-space tests
or
- structural-transition tests

This distinction is necessary for disciplined interpretation.

---

## Practical use

This baseline contract should be used to:

- establish calm-regime expectations
- prevent overreading of harmless modulation
- support conformance review
- support later activation claims
- preserve disciplined separation between signal richness and true structural change

---

## Closing statement

A protocol that cannot remain stable under admissible non-transition conditions is not ready to claim meaningful transition sensitivity.

A compact final formulation:

> The non-transition baseline contract protects T-Protocol from mistaking signal modulation for structural activation and preserves a disciplined calm regime against which real transition can later be judged.
