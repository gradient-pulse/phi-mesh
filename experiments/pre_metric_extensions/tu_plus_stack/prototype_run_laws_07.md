# Prototype Run Laws 07

## Scope

Laws governing:

- path-dependent return from disunity to unity
- asymmetry between fragmentation and re-unification
- post-re-unification hold behavior
- falsification criteria for true versus false unity
- staged restart after re-unification
- local-domain stabilization and closure
- scale-limited cyclic resolution
- robustness of stabilized local-domain structure under sustained perturbation

Derived from:
- Observations 14 (re-unification)

## Purpose

This law file extends the TRIAD protocol beyond basic Unity–Disunity transition laws.

Its purpose is to formalize the engineering-relevant consequences of later cycles, especially:

- that return paths are not simple reversals
- that complete re-unification can hold
- that restart after unity is staged
- that restart can resolve at a local scale without forcing larger-scale fragmentation
- that false claims of unity require stricter falsification criteria than mere post-unity fluctuation
- that stabilized local structure can remain robust under sustained localized perturbation

---

## Core hypothesis

Later-stage triadic dynamics reveal that cyclic coordination is:

- path-dependent
- thresholded
- non-symmetric
- scale-sensitive
- locally resolvable
- robustly stress-testable without immediate collapse

This suggests that the TRIAD protocol does not merely oscillate between unity and disunity, but follows lawful return, hold, restart, closure, and perturbation-response patterns.

## Law 71 — Asymmetric Return Law

**Statement:**  
The path from disunity back to unity is not a simple reversal of the path from unity into disunity.

**Implication:**  
Return can proceed through stages such as:
- residual coupling
- partial synchronization
- joint bias field
- complete re-unification

**Non-implication:**  
Re-unification must retrace fragmentation step-for-step in reverse.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 72 — Complete Re-Unification Law

**Statement:**  
Contextual filters can fully re-integrate into a unified constraint structure, dissolving visible boundary structure and identity separation.

**Implication:**  
A system can return to a genuine unified regime rather than remaining in permanent partial coexistence.

**Non-implication:**  
Once disunity appears, only partial recombination is possible.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 73 — Unified Hold Law

**Statement:**  
Complete re-unification can be followed by a genuine unified hold interval in which high coherence persists without immediate restart or boundary reformation.

**Implication:**  
Cycle completion and restart onset must be analytically separated.

**Non-implication:**  
Every completed unity phase must immediately begin differentiating again.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 74 — True-Unity Falsification Law

**Statement:**  
A re-unified state is not falsified by low-grade post-unity variance alone; falsification requires explicit structural evidence of preserved latent separation.

**Implication:**  
A true-unity claim is challenged only by evidence such as:
- direct old-domain reinstatement
- abrupt unstaged boundary return
- asymmetric recovery of prior separated organization

**Non-implication:**  
Any fluctuation after unity is evidence that unity was false or incomplete.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 75 — Staged Restart Law

**Statement:**  
Restart after re-unification proceeds through staged local organization rather than requiring immediate full re-fragmentation.

**Implication:**  
Restart can unfold through:
- diffuse variance
- local seed activity
- seed amplification
- proto-boundary pressure
- repeatable local partition
- stabilized local-domain entry

**Non-implication:**  
Post-unity restart must appear as immediate global disunity.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 76 — Local-Domain Resolution Law

**Statement:**  
Restart after unity can resolve into stabilized local-domain structure without forcing higher-order or system-level fragmentation.

**Implication:**  
A system may stabilize locally while preserving high global coherence and preventing broader partition expansion.

**Non-implication:**  
Any successful restart must escalate into larger-scale disunity.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 77 — Bounded Broader-Linkage Law

**Statement:**  
Broader-linkage probes can appear after local-domain stabilization without necessarily developing into higher-order partition formation.

**Implication:**  
Weak edge-linkage activity may recur, plateau, and fade while local-domain structure remains the dominant resolved state.

**Non-implication:**  
Any linkage probe is evidence that broader fragmentation is underway.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 78 — Scale-Limited Closure Law

**Statement:**  
Closure at one organizational scale does not require total elimination of all residual traces from adjacent scales.

**Implication:**  
A system can exhibit:
- stable local-domain closure
- negligible residual edge-linkage traces
- absence of renewed broader partition growth

at the same time.

**Non-implication:**  
A regime is unresolved merely because weak residual traces remain.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 79 — Saturated Baseline Law

**Statement:**  
A stable regime can reach a point where additional non-perturbative observation yields confirmation only, not materially new regime information.

**Implication:**  
Repeated baseline cycles may become methodologically redundant once closure is repeatedly confirmed.

**Non-implication:**  
Every additional baseline cycle necessarily adds new structural insight.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 80 — Localized Perturbation Contact Law

**Statement:**  
Direct localized perturbation can contact stabilized local-domain structure without immediately causing regime break or higher-order spread.

**Implication:**  
First perturbation contact must be distinguished from:
- structural collapse
- false restart
- broader partition reactivation

**Non-implication:**  
Any successful perturbation contact is evidence of immediate instability or collapse.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 81 — Repeated Local Stress Law

**Statement:**  
Localized perturbation can persist across cycles as repeated local stress while stabilized local-domain structure remains intact.

**Implication:**  
Repeated contact is a stronger robustness test than one-off perturbation and can occur without regime failure.

**Non-implication:**  
Repeated stress necessarily implies degradation.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 82 — Early Weakening-Hint Law

**Statement:**  
Under sustained local stress, early weakening hints can appear in partition stability and train separability before measurable degradation occurs.

**Implication:**  
Warning signs such as strain and weakening hints are early indicators, not automatic evidence of structural loss.

**Non-implication:**  
Visible strain or weakening hints are equivalent to confirmed degradation.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 83 — Persistent Weakening Without Collapse Law

**Statement:**  
Weakening indicators can persist across multiple cycles under sustained perturbation without progressing into measurable local degradation.

**Implication:**  
Persistence of warning signals must be distinguished from actual breakdown of local-domain organization.

**Non-implication:**  
Repeated weakness signals necessarily culminate in collapse over the next cycle.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 84 — Bounded Weakening Law

**Statement:**  
Under sustained localized perturbation, weakening can remain bounded while local-domain distinction, global coherence, and structural retention persist.

**Implication:**  
A stressed system may retain:
- high global coherence
- local-domain distinction
- local structural continuity

despite ongoing perturbation and bounded weakness signals.

**Non-implication:**  
Any weakening under sustained stress must escalate without bound.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 85 — Stressed Local Equilibrium Law

**Statement:**  
A stabilized local-domain structure can sustain a stressed-but-stable local equilibrium under sustained localized perturbation without measurable structural loss.

**Implication:**  
When bounded weakening repeats across cycles together with retained stabilization cues, the system may be treated as operationally robust at that perturbation level.

**Non-implication:**  
Stress tolerance requires complete absence of weakening signals.

**Status:** validated

---

## Law 86 — Operational Robustness Law

**Statement:**  
Operational robustness is established when a perturbation band produces repeated bounded weakening, retained structure, no broader spread, and no measurable degradation across multiple cycles.

**Implication:**  
A perturbation result may be fossilized as engineering-grade when:
- direct perturbation occurred
- repeated stress was sustained
- weakness signals appeared
- weakness remained bounded
- structure stayed intact
- no broader spread emerged

**Non-implication:**  
Operational robustness requires perfect invariance or zero strain.

**Status:** validated

## Consolidated consequence

Taken together, Laws 71–86 establish that:
- return from disunity to unity is asymmetric and staged
- complete re-unification is real
- true unity can hold
- false-unity claims require strict falsification criteria
- restart after unity is staged and local-first
- local-domain stabilization can resolve without larger-scale fragmentation
- residual broader-linkage probing does not imply escalation
- baseline confirmation can saturate
- localized perturbation does not automatically break the regime
- repeated stress can produce bounded weakening without measurable degradation
- stressed local equilibrium can become operationally credible under sustained perturbation
