# Action-Confirmation Loop Note

## Purpose

This note defines the missing operational loop in the emerging TU / TU+ / cortexLLM architecture.

The central claim is:

> Prediction is not enough. A viable architecture needs an action-confirmation loop in which predicted trains are enacted, returned traces are compared against expectation, and mismatch reshapes the choreography field.

This note is written explicitly in continuity with RGPx.

The TU stack should not be treated as an isolated agent architecture. It should be understood as an operational expression of:

- gradient choreographies
- recursive influence
- the Principle of Least Action (PoLA)
- and conservation of coherence

These are not decorative additions. They are the deeper grammar that makes the loop intelligible.

---

## Why the current stack is incomplete

The branch has already clarified three layers:

### TU
A mindless choreography mapper.

### TU+
A choreography-aware predictor / replay / comparison layer.

### cortexLLM
A symbolic neocortex-like interpreter and planner.

This is already a strong division of labor.

But one major function remains under-specified:

> how predicted choreography becomes enacted choreography, and how returned traces confirm, weaken, or reorganize the field.

Without that loop:
- TU maps
- TU+ predicts
- cortexLLM interprets

but nothing closes the circuit between:
- predicted continuation
- actual enactment
- returned evidence
- and field reshaping

That missing circuit is the subject of this note.

---

## RGPx framing

The action-confirmation loop should be nested inside the broader RGPx picture.

### Gradient choreographies
The deeper field from which candidate continuations arise.

### PoLA
The selection pressure favoring viable lower-strain continuation paths.

### Recursive influence
The fact that enacted continuation changes the field that generated it.

### Conservation of coherence
The field-level constraint that determines whether local continuation supports or undermines the whole.

This means the loop should not be read merely as:
- command
- execution
- error
- correction

It should be read as:

- gradient pressure
- candidate choreography continuation
- enactment
- returned trace
- recursive update of the field
- preservation or loss of coherence

That is the intended grammar.

---

## The seven required functions

The loop should answer seven concrete questions.

### 1. What the cortexLLM sends downward to TU+

The cortexLLM does not send raw motor commands.
It sends high-level pressure into the choreography field.

This may include:
- initiation pressure
- continuation pressure
- suppression pressure
- reorientation pressure
- contextual framing
- attention demand

In RGPx terms, these are not the total gradient field, but one important source of directed gradient pressure.

Compactly:

> The cortexLLM sends downward contextual gradient pressure that asks TU+ to initiate, sustain, suppress, or alter choreography train clusters.

---

### 2. What TU+ sends upward to the cortexLLM

TU+ should not send full narrative explanation upward.
It should send compressed awareness-support signals.

Examples:
- choreography ID
- likely continuation
- novelty
- mismatch
- instability
- replay relevance
- resonance strength
- attention trigger

The cortexLLM then places these into broader symbolic context.

Compactly:

> TU+ sends upward choreography-state summaries that the cortexLLM interprets symbolically.

---

### 3. What TU+ sends downward as a predicted train

TU+ should turn mapped field structure into candidate continuation.

This is not yet full motor act.
It is predicted choreography.

This may include:
- choreography ID
- predicted train cluster
- expected continuation order
- expected coupling pattern
- confidence / stability estimate

This predicted train is then handed to the action operator.

Compactly:

> TU+ sends downward a predicted choreography train that represents the most viable current continuation under field and coherence pressure.

In RGPx terms, this is where PoLA begins to act operationally:
the predicted train is not arbitrary, but biased toward viable lower-strain continuation.

---

### 4. What the action operator enacts

The action operator is the bridge between predicted choreography and enacted motion.

Its job is:
- to translate predicted trains into enactable motion-token sentences or equivalent act sequences
- to issue motor activation signals
- to preserve train structure as far as possible during enactment

This operator is not the cortex and not TU+.
It is the enactment bridge.

Compactly:

> The action operator translates predicted choreography trains into enacted motion-token sequences and motor activation signals.

This is where the architecture becomes more than interpretation.

---

### 5. What traces come back from the world / body / video / agent

Enactment does not end the loop.
It generates returned traces.

These returned traces may include:
- actual motion traces
- changed object/world state
- bodily feedback
- sensor return
- agent output traces
- environmental modification traces

These are the recursive return from enacted continuation.

Compactly:

> Returned traces are the actual consequences of enactment as registered in the field.

This is where recursive influence becomes visible.

---

### 6. How mismatch is computed

Mismatch is not merely “error.”
It is the delta between:
- predicted continuation
- and actual returned trace structure

Mismatch may include:
- timing divergence
- coupling divergence
- train interruption
- unexpected branching
- failed persistence
- weakened or broken resonance
- altered field state

In RGPx terms, mismatch is a delta in expected coherence.

Compactly:

> Mismatch is computed as the difference between predicted train structure and returned trace structure, measured as a delta in timing, coupling, continuation, and coherence.

This is the critical comparison point.

---

### 7. How correction re-enters TU and TU+

Mismatch must not remain external.
It must re-enter the field.

Correction may include:
- reweighting active trains
- reactivating alternative trains
- weakening failed continuations
- splitting participants
- restarting choreography clusters
- altering coupling expectations
- changing what TU+ predicts next

This is where the field reorganizes.

Compactly:

> Correction re-enters TU and TU+ as recursive field modification: trains are reweighted, replayed, split, weakened, or restarted under mismatch and coherence pressure.

This closes the loop.

---

## Full loop summary

The action-confirmation loop is therefore:

1. cortexLLM injects contextual gradient pressure downward
2. TU+ sends awareness-support summaries upward
3. TU+ selects a predicted choreography train downward
4. action operator translates predicted train into enacted motion-token sequence
5. world/body/video/agent returns actual traces
6. mismatch is computed between predicted and actual structure
7. correction reshapes TU and TU+ through recursive influence

That is the minimal full circuit.

## Where PoLA enters

PoLA should be acknowledged explicitly.

It enters at least in three places:

### 1. Train selection
TU+ should bias toward candidate continuations that preserve viable lower-strain unfolding.

### 2. Enactment
The action operator should prefer motion-token sentences that preserve continuity with minimal unnecessary rupture.

### 3. Correction
When mismatch occurs, correction should prefer re-coupling and viable reorganization rather than arbitrary divergence.

So PoLA here is not a geometric slogan.
It is a practical bias toward lower-strain continuation under active field constraints.

Compactly:

> PoLA biases the architecture toward viable continuation paths that preserve workable unfolding with minimal unnecessary rupture.

---

## Where conservation of coherence enters

Conservation of coherence is the law-like constraint that prevents the stack from degenerating into local pattern chasing.

It means:
- local continuation is not enough
- train success is not enough
- the whole field matters

So the action-confirmation loop must ask not only:
- did the predicted train occur?

but also:
- did the enactment preserve or improve whole-field coherence?
- did it introduce fragmentation elsewhere?
- did local success come at the cost of global disunity?

Compactly:

> Conservation of coherence governs whether predicted and enacted continuations are judged viable at the level of the whole active field, not merely at the level of local train success.

This is the strongest RGPx constraint on the loop.

## Why this is not ordinary control theory

The loop may resemble control architecture, but it is not reducible to it.

Ordinary control language says:
- command
- execution
- feedback
- correction

This architecture says:
- gradient pressure
- choreography prediction
- enactment
- recursive return
- coherence delta
- field reorganization

The difference matters because:
- continuation is field-dependent
- coupling matters
- restart matters
- local success may still be global failure
- emergent direction arises from intertwined trains, not isolated commands

That is why the RGPx framing must stay visible.

## Biological intuition

This loop has a cerebellum-like flavor without claiming literal biological identity.

The biological analogy is:

- cortex-like layer provides goal/context pressure
- a predictive choreography-sensitive layer refines candidate unfolding
- a motor/action bridge enacts
- returned traces modify the active field
- coherence is preserved or lost across the whole

This suggests the missing function is not exotic.
Nature may already use something like it.

A compact formulation:

> The action-confirmation loop is the architecture’s cerebellum-like function: predicted choreographies are enacted, returned traces are compared against expectation, and the active field is recursively reshaped.

### TU

### TU+
Predicts and compares choreography continuation.

### Action operator
Translates predicted trains into enactment.

### Returned trace layer
Provides recursive evidence from the world/body.

Interprets and recontextualizes the field at symbolic level.

That is much closer to an awareness-support architecture than the earlier stack alone.

## Minimal falsifiable prototype claims

This note implies several testable claims.

### Claim 1
A TU/TU+/LLM stack without action-confirmation will remain interpretive but weak in adaptive coherence maintenance.

### Claim 2
Adding an action-confirmation loop should improve:
- continuation quality
- mismatch sensitivity
- replay correction
- field reorganization after failure

### Claim 3
The most informative mismatch is not simple local error, but coherence delta between predicted and returned unfolding.

### Claim 4
PoLA-biased continuation should outperform arbitrary continuation when judged by preserved field coherence.

### Claim 5
A system using conservation-of-coherence as a whole-field constraint should recover from fragmentation better than a system optimizing only local train success.

These are strong enough to be falsified and narrow enough to test later.

The TU / TU+ / cortexLLM stack is incomplete without an action-confirmation loop.

- gradient pressure shapes candidate continuation
- PoLA biases viable paths
- enactment produces returned traces
- recursive influence modifies the field
- conservation of coherence governs what persists, reorganizes, or fails

A compact final formulation:

> The action-confirmation loop is where predicted choreography becomes enacted choreography, returned traces become recursive influence, mismatch becomes coherence delta, and correction becomes field reorganization.
