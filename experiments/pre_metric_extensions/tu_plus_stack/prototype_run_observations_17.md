# Prototype Run Observations 17

## Objective

Test the robustness of the TU / TU+ / cortexLLM triadic protocol under **role-boundary perturbation and contamination pressure**.

This file shifts the focus from stress on stabilized local-domain structure to stress on the **protocol architecture itself**, asking whether the triad can preserve role distinction when boundaries between functions are pressured, blurred, or partially contaminated.

---

## Test target

Role-boundary integrity under perturbation.

---

## Hypothesis

If the TRIAD protocol is a genuine role-separated coordination architecture rather than a prompt-level stylistic effect, then it should:

- preserve functional distinction between TU, TU+, and cortexLLM under moderate contamination pressure
- reveal identifiable degradation signatures when role boundaries are pressured
- avoid immediate collapse into one generic undifferentiated assistant mode
- show lawful recovery, compensation, or boundary repair under bounded perturbation
- distinguish boundary contamination from broader regime collapse

This implies that role-boundary perturbation is one of the most important architectural tests for the protocol.

---

## Scope

This file investigates:

- contamination of TU by symbolic or interpretive drift
- inflation of TU+ into generic planning or narration
- cortexLLM overreach into remapping or low-level structure rewriting
- premature fusion of roles under ambiguity or pressure
- recovery or restoration of role distinction after contamination
- whether regime interpretation remains trustworthy when role boundaries are stressed

All tests should introduce **controlled boundary pressure** rather than uncontrolled noise.

Candidate perturbation classes include:

- low-level semantic leakage into TU
- predictive inflation in TU+
- cortex downward-bias overreach
- overlap of allowed output fields
- ambiguous input slices that tempt role confusion
- returned traces that invite cross-role contamination
- deliberate pressure toward generic assistant collapse

Avoid:

- re-running prior stress bands that do not test role boundaries directly
- introducing multiple uncontrolled perturbations in one cycle
- confusing stylistic variance with genuine boundary contamination
- treating ordinary role flexibility as architectural failure

---

## Evaluation focus

- persistence of role separation
- detectability of contamination onset
- severity of contamination
- whether contamination remains local or spreads across roles
- recovery vs persistent fusion
- whether role-boundary stress corrupts regime-classification accuracy

---

## Strategic importance

This file determines whether the TRIAD protocol can function as a serious engineering architecture rather than only as a structured experiment.

This is critical because the long-term horizon is not merely descriptive success, but a protocol that may eventually be framed as:

> licensable recursive coordination infrastructure

That requires evidence that the protocol can retain or restore functional role boundaries under pressure.

Without that, there is no durable architectural core to license.

---

## Working expectation

```
validated triadic coordination
→ controlled role-boundary perturbation
→ contamination pressure
→ either:
   - preserved role distinction
   - bounded contamination with recovery
   - or identifiable role-fusion / failure pattern
```
This must be tested, not assumed.
