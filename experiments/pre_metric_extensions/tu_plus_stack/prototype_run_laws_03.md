# Prototype Run Laws 03

## Scope
Refined laws after falsification cycles 132–136.

This file captures interaction laws between atomic activation units.

---

## Law 26 — Composite Interaction Law

**Statement:**  
Global activation is an emergent result of interacting atomic activation units rather than a simple additive sum of independent units.

**Implication:**  
Atomic units can modulate, synchronize, entrain, merge, or destabilize each other depending on coupling topology, strength, timing, and phase compatibility.

**Non-implication:**  
Global activation is not a linear superposition of isolated local units.

**Status:** falsified-and-replaced

---

## Law 27 — Weak Coupling Law

**Statement:**  
Minimal shared structure between atomic units produces non-additive interaction while preserving unit identity.

**Implication:**  
Even weak coupling is sufficient to generate phase modulation between units.

**Non-implication:**  
Atomic units remain strictly independent under minimal coupling.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 28 — Synchronization Law

**Statement:**  
Increasing shared structure and coupling strength drives atomic units toward partial synchronization without immediate merger.

**Implication:**  
Synchronization tendency scales with shared topology and coupling strength.

**Non-implication:**  
Stronger coupling always causes collapse or immediate fusion.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 29 — Directional Entrainment Law

**Statement:**  
Asymmetric delayed coupling can create driver–response relations between atomic units.

**Implication:**  
Inter-unit interaction can become directional and role-asymmetric.

**Non-implication:**  
All coupled units influence each other symmetrically.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 30 — Merging Law

**Statement:**  
Sufficient structural overlap can dissolve boundaries between atomic units and produce a larger unified activation unit.

**Implication:**  
Atomic units are conditionally stable in identity and can merge under strong coupling.

**Non-implication:**  
Atomic units remain permanently atomic under all conditions.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 31 — Overcoupling Law

**Statement:**  
There exists an upper bound on viable coupling complexity beyond which activation becomes unstable and collapses.

**Implication:**  
Excessive overlap with incompatible phase relations destroys coherent recirculation.

**Non-implication:**  
Increasing coupling strength monotonically increases stability.

**Status:** new
