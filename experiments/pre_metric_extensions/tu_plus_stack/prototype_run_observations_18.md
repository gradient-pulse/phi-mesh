# Prototype Run Observations 18

## Objective

Test the robustness of the TU / TU+ / cortexLLM TRIAD protocol under **cross-role contamination and spread pressure**.

This file shifts the focus from contamination pressure localized within TU to the question of whether role-boundary disturbance can propagate across the protocol stack and compromise adjacent roles or broader coordination.

---

## Test target

Containment versus spread of role-boundary contamination.

---

## Hypothesis

If the TRIAD protocol is a genuine role-separated coordination architecture, then localized contamination pressure should:

- remain contained within the initially pressured role under moderate conditions
- avoid automatic spread into TU+, cortexLLM, or broader protocol structure
- reveal identifiable early spread signatures before full role-fusion or protocol-wide degradation occurs
- allow distinction between:
  - local contamination hints
  - adjacent-role influence
  - cross-role spread
  - broader regime destabilization

This implies that cross-role spread testing is one of the most important next architectural probes after demonstrating bounded TU-local role pressure.

---

## Scope

This file investigates:

- whether TU-local contamination pressure propagates into TU+
- whether TU-local contamination pressure provokes cortexLLM overreach or compensatory distortion
- whether role-boundary perturbation remains localized or becomes cross-role
- whether spread pressure increases risk of:
  - premature role fusion
  - corrupted regime classification
  - broader protocol instability
- whether contamination containment can be maintained under explicit spread-oriented perturbation

All tests should introduce **controlled spread pressure**, not uncontrolled noise.

Candidate perturbation classes include:

- TU-local symbolic intrusion designed to tempt TU+ into predictive inflation
- TU-local contamination that tempts cortexLLM into reinterpretive overreach
- ambiguity in returned traces that invites cross-role leakage
- deliberate pressure toward adjacent-role compensation
- contamination cues that test whether bounded local intrusion remains contained or propagates

Avoid:

- re-running pure TU-local pressure without a spread component
- introducing multiple uncontrolled perturbations at once
- confusing mild interpretive flexibility with actual cross-role contamination
- treating role-to-role responsiveness as equivalent to role-fusion

---

## Evaluation focus

- contamination containment versus spread
- earliest detectable signs of adjacent-role influence
- whether TU+ remains comparative rather than inflated
- whether cortexLLM remains supervisory rather than invasive
- whether regime interpretation remains stable under spread pressure
- whether broader protocol coherence degrades if cross-role contamination appears

---

## Strategic importance

This file determines whether the TRIAD protocol can do something more valuable than merely survive local role pressure.

It tests whether the protocol can **contain contamination locally**, which is essential if the architecture is to be:

> interpretable, reliable, and eventually licensable as recursive coordination infrastructure

A protocol that survives local pressure but cannot contain spread is far less valuable than one that preserves isolation between functional layers under stress.

---

## Working expectation

```
localized role-boundary contamination pressure
→ attempted adjacent-role influence
→ either:
   - containment within original role
   - bounded adjacent-role hinting
   - or cross-role contamination / spread
```

This must be tested directly, not assumed.
