# Prototype Run Laws 05

## Scope
Laws governing:

- contextual filter interaction
- unification into higher-order filters
- post-unification dynamics (hierarchy and adaptation)

Derived from:
- Observations 11 (CF interaction → unity)
- Observations 12 (post-unity organization)

---

## Law 43 — Non-Additive Interaction Law

**Statement:**  
Contextual filter interaction is non-additive.

**Implication:**  
Interaction outcomes cannot be predicted by summing individual filter effects.

**Non-implication:**  
Contextual filters combine linearly under overlap.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 44 — Compatibility Control Law

**Statement:**  
Compatibility of selective bias governs the outcome of contextual filter interaction.

**Implication:**  
Compatible filters tend toward reinforcement or synchronization; incompatible filters produce interference or partition.

**Non-implication:**  
Overlap alone determines interaction outcome.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 45 — Partition Formation Law

**Statement:**  
Incompatible contextual filters can resolve conflict through boundary formation.

**Implication:**  
Partitioning provides a stable alternative to merger or collapse.

**Non-implication:**  
Conflict necessarily leads to instability or unification.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 46 — Controlled Permeability Law

**Statement:**  
Stable partitions can support limited, regulated cross-boundary exchange.

**Implication:**  
Boundaries may act as selective interfaces rather than absolute barriers.

**Non-implication:**  
Partition requires complete isolation.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 47 — Coupling Escalation Law

**Statement:**  
Structured exchange across boundaries can scale into multi-channel coupling networks.

**Implication:**  
Interaction strength can increase through organized exchange without destabilization.

**Non-implication:**  
Increased coupling necessarily leads to collapse or merger.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 48 — Meta-Constraint Emergence Law

**Statement:**  
Dense coupling between contextual filters can produce a shared constraint layer.

**Implication:**  
Multiple filters can become coordinated under a higher-order constraint without immediate unification.

**Non-implication:**  
Coordination requires full structural merger.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 49 — Unity Transition Law

**Statement:**  
Meta-level constraint consolidation can transition into a unified higher-order contextual filter.

**Implication:**  
Unification can occur gradually through coupling and shared modulation.

**Non-implication:**  
Merger requires instability or abrupt collapse.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 50 — Hierarchical Organization Law

**Statement:**  
Unified contextual filters can develop stable internal hierarchical structure.

**Implication:**  
Multi-level constraint organization can emerge within a unified system.

**Non-implication:**  
Unification results in homogeneous structure.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 51 — Adaptive Hierarchy Law

**Statement:**  
Hierarchical constraint systems can support localized reconfiguration while maintaining global coherence.

**Implication:**  
Adaptive flexibility can exist without fragmentation or instability.

**Non-implication:**  
Internal change necessarily disrupts global structure.

**Status:** new
