# Prototype Run Bridge 01

## Purpose

This document formalizes the causal transition from:

- atomic activation units (minimal closed feedback loops)
→ sustained activation
→ contextual filter emergence

Its goal is to prevent conceptual drift and to establish a structurally precise bridge between:

- mechanism (loop)
- regime (activation)
- constraint (contextual filter)

---

## Core chain

```
gradient perturbation
→ local coupling
→ minimal closed feedback loop formation
→ sustained activation
→ persistence over time
→ constraint stabilization
→ contextual filter emergence
```

### Layer 1 — Minimal Closed Feedback Loop

**Definition:**
A minimal closed feedback loop is the smallest topologically closed propagation structure capable of sustaining recirculating activation.

**Necessary conditions:**
-	closed topology (no open edges)
-	sufficient local connectivity
-	recirculation within temporal coherence window

**Non-implications:**
-	does not imply persistence over time
-	does not imply influence beyond its local structure
-	does not imply contextual constraint formation

---

### Layer 2 — Sustained Activation

**Definition:**
Sustained activation is a regime in which a minimal loop maintains recirculating activity over time within its coherence bounds.

**Necessary conditions:**
-	intact minimal loop
-	feedback reinforcement within temporal window
-	absence of destabilizing interference

**Non-implications:**
-	does not imply long-term persistence
-	does not imply structural influence on external dynamics
-	does not imply contextual filter formation

---

### Layer 3 — Persistent Activation

**Definition:**
Persistent activation is sustained activation that remains stable over an extended temporal interval relative to local decay timescales.

**Necessary conditions:**
-	sustained activation maintained over time
-	resistance to small perturbations
-	internal phase coherence

**Non-implications:**
-	does not guarantee downstream influence
-	does not automatically impose constraints on surrounding structure
-	does not necessarily generate a contextual filter

---

### Layer 4 — Constraint Stabilization

**Definition:**
Constraint stabilization occurs when persistent activation begins to shape or restrict the propagation of surrounding gradients.

**Necessary conditions:**
-	persistent activation
-	interaction with surrounding propagation space
-	repeated influence on local flow structure

**Non-implications:**
-	does not require global influence
-	does not imply full system reconfiguration
-	does not yet constitute a contextual filter

---

### Layer 5 — Contextual Filter (CF)

**Definition:**
A contextual filter is a stabilized constraint structure that biases, redirects, or limits future gradient propagation based on prior activation history.

**Necessary conditions:**
-	persistent activation
-	stabilized constraint effect on surrounding propagation
-	reproducible influence on subsequent dynamics

**Non-implications:**
-	is not the same as the loop that generated it
-	does not require continuous active reinforcement (may persist transiently)
-	does not imply global dominance

---

### Key distinctions

*Loop vs Activation vs Filter**
-	minimal loop → structural mechanism
-	sustained activation → dynamic regime
-	contextual filter → emergent constraint
