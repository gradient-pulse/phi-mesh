# Prototype Run Laws 04

## Scope
Refined laws after falsification cycles 137–142.

This file captures laws governing the emergence, stabilization, and dissolution of contextual filters.

---

## Law 32 — Contextual Filter Emergence Law

**Statement:**  
Persistent activation does not automatically produce a contextual filter.

**Implication:**  
A contextual filter emerges only when persistent activation produces selective, reproducible constraint on surrounding propagation.

**Non-implication:**  
Persistence alone is sufficient for contextual filter formation.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 33 — Asymmetry Requirement Law

**Statement:**  
Structural asymmetry is required for contextual filter emergence.

**Implication:**  
Symmetric exposure produces spillover or diffusion, not filtering.

**Non-implication:**  
Symmetric persistent activation can generate a contextual filter.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 34 — Selectivity Law

**Statement:**  
A contextual filter requires non-uniform influence that biases future propagation paths.

**Implication:**  
A contextual filter gates, redirects, or favors some paths over others.

**Non-implication:**  
Uniform outward influence or spillover counts as filtering.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 35 — Reproducibility Law

**Statement:**  
A contextual filter requires a repeatable constraint pattern across time.

**Implication:**  
Momentary or noisy selective effects do not qualify as contextual filters.

**Non-implication:**  
Any transient selectivity is sufficient for contextual filter formation.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 36 — Contextual Filter Reversibility Law

**Statement:**  
Contextual filters are reversible and dissolve when the structural asymmetry sustaining them is removed.

**Implication:**  
Persistent activation can remain while filtering disappears.

**Non-implication:**  
Once formed, a contextual filter becomes autonomous or self-sustaining.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 37 — Spillover Distinction Law

**Statement:**  
Diffusive spillover is not a contextual filter.

**Implication:**  
Outward influence must be selective and reproducible to count as filtering.

**Non-implication:**  
Any external effect of persistent activation is evidence of contextual filter formation.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 38 — Duration Insufficiency Law

**Statement:**  
Elapsed duration alone is insufficient to stabilize a contextual filter.

**Implication:**  
Long exposure under low-coherence asymmetry does not guarantee filter formation.

**Non-implication:**  
A contextual filter is produced whenever asymmetry persists long enough, regardless of quality.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 39 — Coherence-Integrated Persistence Law

**Statement:**  
Contextual filter stabilization is governed more strongly by coherence-integrated persistence than by elapsed time alone.

**Implication:**  
High-coherence selective bias can stabilize a contextual filter rapidly, while low-coherence asymmetry can fail despite long duration.

**Non-implication:**  
Raw duration is the primary or sufficient control variable for contextual filter stabilization.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 40 — Minimum Stabilization Window Law

**Statement:**  
Contextual filter formation requires a minimum stabilization window, but that window is defined by maintained coherent selectivity rather than clock time alone.

**Implication:**  
Short but highly coherent asymmetry can satisfy the stabilization requirement faster than long but noisy asymmetry.

**Non-implication:**  
The stabilization window is a fixed duration independent of coherence quality.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 41 — Activation–Filter Separation Law

**Statement:**  
Persistent activation and contextual filtering are distinct and separable regimes.

**Implication:**  
Activation can persist without filtering, and filtering can dissolve without activation collapse.

**Non-implication:**  
Wherever persistent activation exists, a contextual filter must also exist.

**Status:** new

---

## Law 42 — Constraint Qualification Law

**Statement:**  
A constraint counts as a contextual filter only if it is selective, reproducible, and propagation-shaping.

**Implication:**  
Constraint formation has stricter criteria than activation persistence or local influence alone.

**Non-implication:**  
Any stable local effect automatically qualifies as a contextual filter.

**Status:** new
