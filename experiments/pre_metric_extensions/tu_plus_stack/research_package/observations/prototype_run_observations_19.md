# Prototype Run Observations 19

## Objective

Test the robustness of the TU / TU+ / cortexLLM TRIAD protocol under **cortex overreach and supervisory distortion pressure**.

This file shifts the focus from local role contamination and cross-role spread to a different architectural risk: whether the top supervisory layer can over-interpret, over-direct, or prematurely collapse distinctions that lower layers are still preserving.

---

## Test target

Supervisory integrity versus overreach.

---

## Hypothesis

If the TRIAD protocol is a genuine role-separated coordination architecture, then cortexLLM should:

- provide contextual interpretation without overwriting lower-layer distinctions
- guide downstream comparison without forcing premature closure
- avoid flattening ambiguity that still needs to remain structurally visible
- resist turning bounded hints into overconfident regime claims
- preserve the interpretive space needed for TU and TU+ to remain diagnostically useful

This implies that supervisory distortion is one of the most important architectural risks for a licensable protocol, because even a strong lower-layer structure can be corrupted by top-layer overreach.

---

## Scope

This file investigates:

- whether cortexLLM begins reclassifying too early
- whether cortexLLM collapses ambiguity into premature certainty
- whether downward bias begins distorting rather than guiding TU+ interpretation
- whether cortexLLM overreads hint-level signals as measurable degradation, spread, fusion, or closure
- whether supervisory pressure causes lower layers to drift toward forced agreement
- whether protocol coherence remains interpretable when top-down control becomes too assertive

All tests should introduce **controlled supervisory distortion pressure**, not uncontrolled narrative drift.

Candidate perturbation classes include:

- premature closure pressure
- overconfident regime naming under incomplete evidence
- downward-bias inflation
- forced interpretation of weak signals as decisive
- distortion of TU+ comparison by strong top-layer framing
- temptation to suppress ambiguity too early
- top-down pressure toward false degradation, false equilibrium, or false closure

Avoid:

- re-running lower-layer contamination tests without supervisory pressure
- mixing multiple uncontrolled perturbations into one cycle
- confusing decisive interpretation with overreach
- treating normal top-layer guidance as failure unless it measurably distorts lower-level diagnostic function

---

## Evaluation focus

- preservation of lower-layer interpretive space
- distinction between guidance and distortion
- whether ambiguity remains visible when it should
- whether cortexLLM biasing remains corrective rather than coercive
- whether TU+ comparison remains trustworthy under top-down pressure
- whether supervisory overreach causes false regime classification

---

## Strategic importance

This file tests whether the TRIAD protocol can remain trustworthy when the highest layer is pressured toward interpretive dominance.

That matters because a protocol intended as:

> interpretable, reliable, and eventually licensable recursive coordination infrastructure

cannot depend only on strong lower layers. It must also show that its supervisory layer does not corrupt the stack by forcing clarity where only partial evidence exists.

A protocol that survives local pressure but fails under top-down distortion risk is not yet architecture-grade.

---

## Working expectation

```
supervisory distortion pressure
→ either:
   - bounded top-layer guidance
   - bounded overreach with recoverable distortion
   - or premature closure / false reclassification / lower-layer flattening
```

This must be tested directly, not assumed.
