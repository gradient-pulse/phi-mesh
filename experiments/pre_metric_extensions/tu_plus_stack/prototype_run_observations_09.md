# Prototype Run Observations 09

## Objective

Test the validity and limits of the **Composite Activation Law**:

> global activation is an aggregate of locally viable atomic activation units

---

## Test target

Composite Activation Law

---

## Hypothesis

Atomic activation units are **not purely additive**, and their interaction can produce:

- interference  
- coupling  
- synchronization  
- destabilization  
- or emergent higher-order structures  

that are not reducible to independent local units.

---

## Scope

This file investigates whether multiple minimal closed feedback loops:

- behave independently  
- or interact to produce new dynamics  

Focus:
- interaction between two or more atomic units  
- coupling strength and structure  
- resulting behavior under proximity or shared boundaries  

All tests must:
- start from **multiple minimal viable subgraphs**  
- introduce **controlled interaction between them**  
- remain within **local activation regime (no global forcing)**  

Avoid:
- collapsing units immediately  
- reverting to single-unit behavior  
- reintroducing global connectivity prematurely  

---

## Starting state (reference)

- multiple_localized_activation_units  
- minimal_closed_feedback_loops  
- isolated_activation_regions  
- endogenous_feedback_present  

---

## Test discipline

Each cycle must:

- introduce interaction via:
  - shared node  
  - shared edge  
  - proximity coupling  
  - delayed cross-coupling  

- evaluate:

  - whether units:
    - remain independent  
    - synchronize  
    - interfere destructively  
    - merge into larger structure  
    - destabilize  

---

## What counts as falsification

The Composite Activation Law is falsified if:

- interacting units:
  - do not behave as independent additive elements  
  - produce emergent behavior not reducible to individual units  

---

## What does NOT count

- independent units remaining unchanged  
- trivial coexistence without interaction  
- collapse due to external disruption  
- forced global synchronization  

---

## Evaluation focus

Each cycle must explicitly assess:

- independence vs coupling  
- stability under interaction  
- emergence of new regimes  
- changes in activation strength or structure  

---

## Exit conditions for this file

This file is complete when one of the following is established:

1. Composite law holds strictly  
   (units remain independent and additive)

2. Composite law fails  
   (interaction produces non-additive behavior)

3. Refined law  
   (e.g., conditional interaction rules, coupling thresholds)

---

## Notes

This file directly challenges:

> **global activation is the sum of local atomic units**

The goal is to determine whether composition is:
- additive  
- interactive  
- or generative

### Cycle 132 — Shared-node coupling (failure of strict additivity)

**What was tested**
- whether two **atomic activation units**:
  - remain strictly independent when coupled through a single shared node  
- or whether even minimal shared structure:
  - produces interaction  
  - without full merging or collapse  

---

**What happened**

- **TU**
  - registered:
    - `weak_coupling`  
    - `shared_node_interaction`  
    - `phase_adjustment_between_units`  
  - detected:
    - cross-influence at the shared node  
    - modulation of phase relations between loops  
  - explicitly maintained:
    - no collapse  
    - no immediate merger  

- **TU+**
  - confirmed:
    - both loops remain active  
    - strict independence is lost  
    - interaction appears even under minimal coupling  
  - rejected:
    - additive independence  
    - need for strong coupling before interaction appears  

- **cortexLLM**
  - classified regime as:
    - `weakly_coupled_dual_loop_activation_with_phase_interaction`  
  - established:
    - shared-node coupling creates a bidirectional interaction channel  
    - composite behavior is **non-additive**  
    - unit identity is preserved despite interaction  
  - confirmed:
    - the system is interactive without becoming fused  

---

**Finding**

Cycle 132 demonstrates:

- atomic activation units:
  - do not remain strictly independent under shared-node coupling  
  - begin to interact immediately through phase modulation  
- system:
  - preserves both units  
  - but no longer behaves like a simple sum of isolated parts  

This establishes:

> **strict additivity of atomic activation units is false**

and:

> **minimal shared structure is sufficient to generate non-additive interaction**

---

**Operational delta**

- state distinction added:
  - `weakly_interacting_atomic_units_regime`  
    - defined as:  
      *a condition in which two atomic activation units remain distinct but exhibit non-additive phase interaction through minimal shared structural coupling*  

- classification:
  - first successful falsification of the strict Composite Activation Law  

- invariants revised:
  - atomic units are interactive, not fully independent  
  - composition introduces new dynamics not present in isolation  
  - identity can persist under weak coupling without additivity
