# Emission as Token Analogy

## Purpose

This note captures a key engineering bridge between the LLM path and the proto temporal-unit path.

The aim is not to force the temporal unit into transformer language, but to preserve a useful lesson from what made LLMs successful:

> do not engineer intelligence directly; engineer a simple update-bearing unit and the local rules by which larger structure can emerge.

In the temporal-unit setting, the best current analogue of the token is not the participant itself, but the **participant emission**.

---

## Why this analogy matters

Transformers did not begin by directly encoding:
- meaning
- reasoning
- truth
- intelligence

They began with:
- tokens
- contextual coupling
- repeated updates
- scale

From that, higher-order behavior emerged.

The temporal unit should likely be approached in the same spirit.

That means we should look for:
- the smallest processable unit
- the persistent process that generates it
- the local coupling rules
- the conditions under which larger structure emerges

This note proposes the following bridge:

- **token** ≈ **participant emission**
- **persistent hidden process** ≈ **vertical choreography**

---

## Core analogy

### In the LLM path
- token = minimal processable unit
- hidden/contextual state = persistent evolving process
- attention = coupling mechanism
- prediction/update = iterative transformation

### In the temporal-unit path
- emission = minimal processable unit
- vertical choreography = persistent evolving process
- horizontal/vertical coupling = temporal interaction mechanism
- swarm update = iterative coherence transformation

So the closest useful analogy is:

> The participant emission is the token-like observable unit entering local coupling and update.

And one level deeper:

> The vertical choreography is the persistent process that produces and contextualizes those emissions.

---

## Participant versus emission

A major clarification already established elsewhere in this branch is:

- participant ≠ event
- participant = vertical choreography
- event/emission = what the participant produces

This note builds directly on that.

The vertical choreography:
- persists through time
- emits traces as it unfolds
- carries continuity, activation, and cycle phase

The emission:
- is the observable update
- enters vertical and horizontal coupling
- is the minimal unit on which local swarm processing can operate

So if we are looking for the temporal-unit equivalent of the token, it is the **emission**, not the participant.

---

## Why emissions matter

An emission is dynamic, not static.

This is a key difference from the token.

A token is usually a discrete symbolic unit.
An emission is:
- temporally situated
- generated by an ongoing participant
- capable of gaining or losing weight through coupling
- part of a live action-bearing field

So the analogy is useful, but not exact.

A good formulation is:

> The token is a static symbolic unit; the participant emission is a dynamic trace of an ongoing choreography-process.

That difference should be preserved in future engineering work.

---

## How emissions gain weight

A crucial emerging insight is that emissions gain weight through two main routes:

### 1. Vertical persistence
An emission matters more when it belongs to a participant whose unfolding:
- persists
- repeats
- remains active across ticks
- continues to emit coherently

### 2. Horizontal coupling
An emission matters more when it couples with emissions from other participants:
- at the same tick
- or within the relevant near-time window

This gives a compact rule:

> An emission gains weight not merely by occurring, but by persisting vertically and resonating horizontally.

And another:

> Horizontal coupling is what turns a local emission into a field-relevant event.

This is likely one of the central engineering rules of the proto temporal unit.

---

## Minimum engineering picture

The simplest buildable grammar now looks like this:

1. a vertical choreography persists as participant
2. the participant emits a trace
3. the trace links vertically to the participant’s history
4. the trace links horizontally to near-time traces from other participants
5. emission weight rises with persistence and coupling
6. weighted emissions bias the next swarm update
7. the LLM reads the resulting weighted field

This is the bridge from LLM-like emergence to temporal-unit emergence.

---

## Consequences for implementation

This analogy suggests the following practical decomposition.

### Persistent layer
- participant = vertical choreography
- carries activation, history, cycle phase, coupling load

### Emission layer
- minimal local update-bearing trace
- timestamped
- participant-linked
- available for vertical and horizontal coupling

### Coupling layer
- vertical coupling = relation to prior emissions from same participant
- horizontal coupling = relation to same/near-time emissions from other participants

### Readout layer
- compressed field summary for LLM interpretation

This layered picture should guide future simulation code.

---

## Emission trains

A useful refinement is to think of a choreography not as a mere list of events, but as a **train of emissions** unfolding through time.

This helps distinguish the temporal-unit path from the token path more clearly.

In the LLM setting:
- tokens gain relevance through effective contextual use

In the temporal-unit setting:
- emissions gain weight through the persistence of their train
- and through synchronization with other trains

So a compact bridge is:

> Tokens are weighted by contextual effectiveness; emissions are weighted by train persistence and cross-train synchronization.

This suggests the following terminology:

- **participant** = vertical choreography / ongoing process
- **emission train** = ordered unfolding of emissions from that participant
- **weight** = function of vertical persistence plus horizontal synchronization
- **coherence** = viable common temporality among trains

“Train” is useful because it emphasizes:
- continuity
- ordered emission
- momentum
- coupling with other unfolding lines
- and possible fading, restart, or derailment

This is a helpful engineering image for future simulation work.

---

## Why this bridge is useful

This analogy keeps the project from becoming mystical or overcomplicated too early.

It reminds us that the path forward is probably not:
- encode agency directly
- encode embodied conduct directly
- encode creativity directly

But rather:
- define the right minimal unit
- define the right local coupling rules
- iterate
- observe what emerges

That is exactly what worked in the LLM path.

---

## Closing statement

The most useful bridge from the LLM path into the temporal-unit path is this:

> The participant emission is the token-like unit of local update; the vertical choreography is the persistent process that generates it.

From this follows a simple and powerful engineering intuition:

> Emission weight arises from vertical persistence plus horizontal coupling.

That is likely one of the cleanest starting points yet for making the temporal unit buildable.
